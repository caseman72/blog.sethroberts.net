I agree with you that it is actually a good thing to say this is good and that is bad, as you often do in your blog. But saying it in a way that implies that anyone who disagrees with you is a bad person or stupid or something like that — that I don’t like and think is unhelpful. The black and white aspect comes into it with false certainty: The statement is too strong as if the issue is really clear when it isn’t. As if there is less room for disagreement than there actually is.

For example, to say a certain graph is bad for reasons X, Y, and Z — that’s fine. That’s really helpful. But to say a certain graph is wrong — as you have never done, as far as I know — isn’t helpful at all. It implies that there are black-and-white rules about making graphs. That’s an unhelpful implication because making graphs is too complicated for that. Statements like that discourage thinking and encourage blind obedience.
