]]> By: Seth Roberts http://blog.sethroberts.net/2011/01/31/fear-of-retaliation-global-warming-and-nutrition/#comment-835511 Fri, 04 Feb 2011 05:27:16 +0000 http://www.blog.sethroberts.net/2011/01/31/fear-of-retaliation-global-warming-and-nutrition/#comment-835511 I’d guess that situations like Peak Oil have been faced thousands of times during human history: A key resource is running out. Until oil was discovered that key resource was whale fat, used for lamps. Sometimes it really does run out before an alternative is found and the society disappears. The book Collapse describes several examples. Much more common, however, is that disaster is averted because innovation allows the society to move to overexploitation of something else. Without plastics for example, a great deal of modern society wouldn’t be possible. Yet you don’t hear about Peak Metal or Peak Wood — the innovation happened before the catastrophists could start scaring people. On the other hand, I believe that overexploitation is really common and the main reason humans migrated all over the globe — to find places where the food supply hadn’t yet been overexploited. Now there is nowhere else to go. Perhaps rising food prices will eventually cause people to start growing food in cities.
