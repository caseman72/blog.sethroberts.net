]]> By: BPT http://blog.sethroberts.net/2011/06/08/how-to-make-kefir-what-i-didnt-know/#comment-916449 Fri, 10 Jun 2011 17:05:21 +0000 http://blog.sethroberts.net/?p=2977#comment-916449 The best way is to use raw whole milk, kefir grains, and allow to ferment for two days or so at room temp. Heating can kill some of the valuable microorganisms, and you don’t want to do that. Also, the higher the ambient temp (to a point), the faster the fermentation process happens.

Store-bought kefir has been pasteurized, so many of the microorganisms that make up the kefir colony have been killed. The resulting kefir is then less nutritionally valuable. Making kefir from the store-bought stuff is not the best technique for this reason.
