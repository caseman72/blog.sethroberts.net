]]> By: Dennis Mangan http://blog.sethroberts.net/2010/05/28/the-unreasonable-effectiveness-of-my-self-experimentation/#comment-440472 Sat, 29 May 2010 15:19:40 +0000 http://www.blog.sethroberts.net/2010/05/28/the-unreasonable-effectiveness-of-my-self-experimentation/#comment-440472 Seth, your article is very good. I particularly thought that your invocation of Veblen and high vs. low status science was insightful. You mentioned the progress made in obstetrics by the relatively low status obstetricians, and I recently came across another example. Until the 1960s, many women who had Rh negative blood type gave birth to stillborn babies, the babies’ condition being known as erythroblastosis fetalis (today, hemolytic disease of the newborn). In 1941, an M.D. named Philip Levine noticed that a woman who had a stillborn baby then proceeded to have a transfusion reaction from blood donated by the baby’s father, and Levine connected the two incidents as related. As a result of his discovery of the Rh blood group, probably millions of lives have been saved. A chance observation by a prepared mind in a field of immediate practicality.
