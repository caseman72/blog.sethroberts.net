]]> By: Blaise Pascal http://blog.sethroberts.net/2007/09/30/marc-andreessens-career-advice/#comment-48081 Mon, 01 Oct 2007 04:55:04 +0000 http://www.blog.sethroberts.net/2007/09/30/marc-andreessens-career-advice/#comment-48081 Conversely, I’ve a friend who, in a similar situation, jumped from a well-paying university tech-support position to move half-way across the country to join a dot-com. Over the past few years he’s seen several of the businesses he’s worked for sell out and close down. He’s happy where he is, but his income isn’t much higher than it was when he started working for the start ups. If he’d passed on the opportunity, he’d have tons of seniority at the university, fewer periods of unemployment, and more security than he’s experienced.

Would he do it again? Probably. But it’s reasonable to make the same risk-assessment and to decide not to leap.
