Eby http://blog.sethroberts.net/2008/08/02/my-humor-research/#comment-202353 Tue, 05 Aug 2008 00:24:01 +0000 http://www.blog.sethroberts.net/2008/08/02/my-humor-research/#comment-202353 Speaking of status, don’t forget to account for social status in laughter: people are much more likely to laugh at jokes made by their superiors than their inferiors, and in the presence of a person of high status, members of the same group will check whether the superior is laughing before laughing themselves.

People are also more likely to laugh aloud if other people are present, and less likely to do so when no-one can hear.

All of these things seem to point to laughter having a social signaling function that goes beyond the simple expression of pleasure.
