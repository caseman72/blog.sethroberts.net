But to claim, as the Gladwell Critique does, that the message of the newly published Outliers is simply that genius takes hard work is a ridiculous distortion. The thesis of the book is rather that success has much less to do with individual merit–understood as natural talent or ability plus hard work–than we generally like to believe, and much more with to do with factors external to the individual: parental upbringing, culture, random opportunity, and arbitrary advantages. This is not a trivial claim, and it is well worth discussing, especially in America. I can’t say now whether Gladwell accurately reports all the social science he relies on, but I found Outliers a very thought-provoking book. (See also his related piece in the current New Yorker.)
