During the prenatal period, the developing human is most vulnerable to any environmental insult, either chemical (pesticides, industrial chemicals) or physical (radiation, ultrasound). The use of prenatal ultrasound has increased dramatically over the past 30 years as has the prevalence of autism. Yet the focus of most research into causes of autism has been primarily genetics and chemical exposures.However, there is considerable research showing cause for concern and you can find abstracts of the studies at http://www.ultrasound-autism.org You can also find Caroline Rodgers excellent powerpoint presentation The Elephant in the Room at http://iacc.hhs.gov/events/2010/full-committee-mtg-slides-Oct22.shtml
Ultrasound may be safe for adults whose body systems are fully developed but it can severely affect developing babies.
